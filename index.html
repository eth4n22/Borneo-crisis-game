DDT Decision: Borneo Crisis

Multiplayer Systems Policy Game

Turn-based simulation based on the Borneo DDT/cat drop story. Each player manages their own crisis for 12 turns. Make strategic decisions to control disease while managing ecological consequences.

📖 How to Play

← Back to Start

🎯 A. Objective

Your goal is to get the highest score (0–100) by managing disease control while protecting your village. You have 12 turns — make each one count!

Balance is essential: disease, ecosystem, housing, food supply, budget, and community trust all affect your final score.

🔄 B. Turn Structure

Every turn follows this simple flow:

  1. Start of Turn: You automatically receive +3 budget. If you chose +10 last turn, you also receive a +3 investment bonus.
  2. Auto-Draw: You automatically draw 1 card at the start of each turn.
  3. Choose One: Either take +10 Budget right now, OR draw 1 extra card. (You cannot do both.)
  4. Play Cards: Click any cards in your hand to select them. Selected cards are queued to play.
  5. Confirm Actions: Press Confirm Actions to resolve your selected cards, then press Next Player to continue.

You can hold up to 6 cards. Most cards cost budget to play.

🏘️ C. What You Are Managing

These are the key variables in the game:

  • Village Health — Your main survival metric. If it reaches 0, your village collapses.
  • Mosquitoes — Drive malaria risk. More mosquitoes = more disease.
  • Malaria — Directly harms village health based on infection levels.
  • Rats — Spread plague AND eat your grain. Too many rats is dangerous.
  • Plague (Rat Disease) — Directly harms village health based on infection levels.
  • Cats — Naturally control rat populations. More cats = fewer rats.
  • Wasps — Beneficial insects that control caterpillars. DDT harms wasps, leading to caterpillar outbreaks.
  • Caterpillars — Eat thatch and damage roofs. Multiply when wasps are low.
  • DDT — Kills mosquitoes but can harm the ecosystem over time.
  • Roof Integrity — Low roofs cause exposure harm to village health.
  • Grain Stores — Low grain causes starvation harm to village health.
  • Public Trust — High trust (70+) gives a resilience bonus. Low trust weakens your actions.
  • Budget — Required to play most cards. Manage it carefully!

🎴 D. The Cards

Here are all the cards you can draw and play:

💧 Spray DDT — Cost: 12

Primary Effect: Instantly reduces mosquitoes and adds DDT to the ecosystem.

Trade-off: High DDT levels can harm cat populations, which may cause rat populations to explode later.

Delay: Ecological effects appear after 2 turns.

Value: Fast, powerful disease control — but use sparingly to avoid ecological backlash.

🐱 Cat Drop — Cost: 15

Primary Effect: Adds cats to the ecosystem to hunt rats.

Trade-off: High cost. Effects are delayed by 1 turn.

Value: Effective long-term rat control. Helps prevent plague and protects grain.

📢 Awareness Campaign — Cost: 10

Primary Effect: Greatly boosts public trust and provides immediate reductions to both malaria and plague.

Trade-off: Also reduces mosquitoes and rats after 1 turn delay.

Value: A solid all-around choice that improves trust and reduces disease pressure.

🏚️ Repair Thatch — Cost: 8

Primary Effect: Instantly improves roof integrity.

Trade-off: Does not affect disease directly.

Value: Essential for protecting against exposure harm. No delay — immediate results.

🌾 Protect Grain — Cost: 6

Primary Effect: Instantly adds grain stores.

Trade-off: Does not affect disease directly.

Value: One of the more affordable cards. Great for preventing starvation harm.

🏥 Medical Aid — Cost: 5

Primary Effect: Instantly reduces both malaria and plague pressure.

Trade-off: Lower cost means lower immediate impact than some options.

Value: Affordable immediate relief from disease pressure.

💰 Bonus Funding — Cost: Free

Primary Effect: Instantly adds +15 to your budget.

Trade-off: Appears less frequently than other cards.

Value: Free budget boost — especially useful when you need funds for expensive cards.

⏱️ E. Delayed Effects & Strategy

  • Plan Ahead: Many card effects don't happen immediately. DDT takes 2 turns; cat drops take 1 turn.
  • Balance Everything: Overusing one option causes problems. DDT kills mosquitoes but harms cats → rats multiply → more disease!
  • Roof & Grain Are Vital: Disease isn't the only threat. Low roofs cause exposure harm. Low grain causes starvation harm.
  • Trust Matters: At 70+ trust, your village gets a +1 health recovery each turn.
  • Investment Strategy: Choosing +10 budget gives you a +3 bonus next turn. Build up your economy or expand your options!

💡 Quick Loop: DDT → fewer mosquitoes → but may → fewer cats → more rats → more plague + grain loss → village hurt!

🏆 F. Winning the Game

Your final score (0–100) comes from bonuses and penalties:

  • Deaths: Keep them low — this is the biggest factor (max +60 bonus)
  • Infection Peak: Lower peak infections earn points (max +20)
  • Outbreak Duration: Shorter outbreaks score better (max +15)
  • Ecological Balance: Avoid eco collapse (cats <20 AND rats >80) for +20
  • Trust & Budget: Small end-game bonuses for high trust and budget
  • Housing & Food: Keep final roof and grain above 30 for +5 each

💰 Budget System

  • Starting Budget: 10
  • Turn Growth: +3 flat at start of each turn
  • Investment Bonus: +3 next turn if you chose +10 budget
  • Auto-Draw: 1 card drawn automatically each turn
  • Choose One: +10 budget OR draw 1 extra card

⚠️ Harm Thresholds

  • Starvation: Only when grain < 50. Formula: floor((40 - grain) / 10) + 1
  • Exposure: Only when roof < 50. Formula: floor((50 - roof) / 10) + 1
  • Malaria Harm: floor(malaria_infected / 25)
  • Plague Harm: floor(rat_disease_infected / 20)
  • Resilience: +1 health recovery if trust ≥ 70

🌿 Ecological Chain (Version 2B)

DDT creates unintended consequences through ecological pathways:

  • DDT → Wasps: DDT harms wasp populations
  • Wasps → Caterpillars: Fewer wasps = caterpillars multiply
  • Caterpillars → Roof: Caterpillars damage thatch roofs
  • DDT → Food Chain: DDT accumulates in prey (hidden toxicity)
  • Toxicity → Cats: Cats sickened by contaminated prey
  • Fewer Cats → Rats: Less predation = rat population growth
  • Rats → Problems: More rats = more plague and grain loss
